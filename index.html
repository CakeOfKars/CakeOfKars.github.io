ITEC442

IoT is network the physical objects that are embedded sensors software and other technologies for purpose of connecting and sharing data with other devices over the internet such as smart door lockers, medical sensors, smart fire alarms, kuri mobile robots and smoke alarms.

IoT architectures often consist of multiple layers and components that work together to facilitate communication, data processing and decision making. Iot architechtures are perception, transport, processing, application and security layers. Perception layer is the device layer that contains sensors and cameras. Transport layer transmits the data to clouds and centers. Processing layer is analyzing the input data. Application layer is groups and tables decoding for humans understand. Security layer contains equipment( malwares and hjacks) , cloud( cloud security) and connection( networks) .

Also, there is a IoT blocks like data analytics, sensor technologies, embedded electronics, energy managements, machine intelligences, cyber securities and cloud computings.

Lab4 Activity- Digitalization

What are the security implications of the digital economy?

The digital economy has a several security implications that require attention. Its benefits, including enhanced connectivity, efficiency and innovation are accompanied by certain security concerns. Here are some key security considerations related to the digital economy; cybersecurity risks, intellectual property protections, geopolitical or national security considerations, regulatory compliance challenges, protection of critical infrastructures, vulnerabilities in the supply chain, data confidentialities and identity theft with fraudulent activities.

What is a fully digital enterprise?

A fully digital enterprise refers to an organization that has successfully transformed its operations, processes and systems to leverage digital technologies throughout the entire value chain. It is an organization that embraces digital transformation holistically and integrates digital technologies into every aspect of its business.

What are the cyber- security challenges/ concerns with a fully digital enterprise?

Cyber- security challenges/ concerns are extended attack surfaces, advanced cyber threats, data breaches with privacy risks, insider threats, lack of security skills with talent, compliance with legal requirements, risk associated with third parties, operational disruptions, organizational issues and disruptive technologies.

What are the cyber security challenges for a bricks and mortar SME wanting to become a digital enterprise?

These challenges are limited expertises in a cybersecurity, extended attack surfaces, budget constraints, legacy systems with infrastructure, awareness with training gaps, third part risks, data protections with privacy, phishings with social engineering, malwares, loss of mobile devices, internal and external attacks.
